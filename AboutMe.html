Hi, I’m Teekshna.

I’m a student, a thinker, and a builder. Cogito Computo is where I explore the strange, wonderful space where philosophy meets code — where we ask: Can machines think? Should they be moral? And who gets to decide?

I love learning about ethics, playing with tech, breaking down big ideas for curious minds (especially mine), and dreaming of a future where machines are smart and self-aware — but not without a conscience.

When I’m not writing or coding, I’m probably dancing, journaling, or questioning everything.

About the Blog

Cogito Computo means “I think, I compute.” It’s inspired by Descartes’ quote — “I think, therefore I am” — reimagined for a world of algorithms, machine learning, and curious teens.

This is a digital notebook for ethical questions, AI wonderings, and messy teenage ideas — all stitched together with logic, heart, and code.
